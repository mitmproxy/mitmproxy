Overview

This tutorial interactively introduces you to mitmproxy and its most common use cases. It will take approximately 10 minutes to complete, assuming you are comfortable on the command-line and familiar with HTTP. By the end of this tutorial, you will know how to:

  1. Navigate mitmproxy's UI using your keyboard.
  2. View HTTP flows and their details, such as HTTP headers.
  3. Intercept and modify requests, before passing them to the server.
  4. Replay previous requests, i.e., send them to the server again.

For this tutorial, we will look at the HTTP requests generated by our demonstration voting app on the right. Let's see if you can use mitmproxy to get your favorite pet some extra votes!
